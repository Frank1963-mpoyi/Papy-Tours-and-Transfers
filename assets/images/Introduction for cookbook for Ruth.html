Yes! Pain perdu is a French dish that literally means "lost bread." It's what many people know as French toast.

Why is it called "lost bread"?

Because traditionally, it was made to use up stale or "lost" bread that would otherwise be thrown away. Soaking it in a mixture of eggs and milk softens the bread, and then its fried until golden.

Basic Pain Perdu Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • Slices of stale bread (like brioche or baguette)

  • 2 eggs

  • 1 cup milk or cream

  • 12 tablespoons sugar

  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)

  • Butter (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. Whisk eggs, milk, sugar, and vanilla.

  2. Dip the bread slices into the mixture and let them soak well.

  3. Fry in a pan with butter until golden on both sides.

  4. Serve warm with powdered sugar, syrup, fruits, or even a scoop of cream.

Variations:

  • Sweet: Add cinnamon, orange zest, or top with berries and honey.

  • Savory: Use less sugar and top with cheese, herbs, or even ham.
